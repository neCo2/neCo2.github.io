tl;dr
If you can afford Wacom, get Wacom. Screen tablets are nice to have, not a must have.

The long and the short of it is that Wacom is expensive, but high quality and long-lasting, while other brands, such as Huion, are half the price of Wacom shit for about 3/4ths of the performance.
Also of note is that in my experience, Huion devices take planned obsolescence to heart. It wasn't even 3 months after my Huion Kamvas Pro 13's guarantee ran out before I noticed some dead pixels at the top of the screen. Turns out that part of the tablet ran extremely hot. However some thermal glue and a couple pieces of aluminium made for a decent enough DIY heatsink to fix that problem. IMAGE

As for the screen vs non-screen tablet debate, it's very much up to personal preference and your wallet. Non-screen tablets take a bit of getting used to, but can actually be quite useful that your hand never covers anything on screen. And the argument for screen tablets is, of course, that it feels a lot more natural.
Personally, it also feels easier to draw long, straight lines. Make of that what you will.
